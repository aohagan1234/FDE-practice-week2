# Discovery Question Generator
**Use the morning the scenario is released, before the live discovery call.**
**Paste in:** The scenario pack in full.

---

## Prompt

```
Read the scenario below carefully. Then produce a structured discovery question guide for a 60-minute client call. Do not include any explanation or preamble.

[PASTE SCENARIO HERE]

---

**SECTION 1 — Assumptions to test before the call**

Table of assumptions already embedded in the scenario that would materially change the agent design if wrong:

| Assumption | Why it matters | What to listen for that breaks it |

---

**SECTION 2 — 60-minute call plan**

Map questions to the following time blocks. For each block, produce the questions and a one-line note on what you are listening for.

**Minutes 0–5 — Context setting**
Goal: establish that this is a work-pattern conversation, not a system audit.
Questions: (2 questions maximum)

**Minutes 5–15 — Broad funnel**
Goal: identify which areas carry the most cognitive load, volume, or coordination pain.
Questions: (3–4 open questions about work patterns, time sinks, and frustration points)
Listen for: categories of work, frequency, context switching, manual handoffs between systems

**Minutes 15–30 — Narrow funnel**
Goal: deep dive into one specific process — the one that showed the most signal in the broad funnel.
Questions: (4–5 questions that walk through a real case, not an SOP)
Listen for: pause points, judgment calls, exception patterns, systems touched

**Minutes 30–45 — Lived vs documented**
Goal: find the gap between how the process is supposed to work and how it actually works.
Questions: (3–4 questions probing for workarounds, deviations, and undocumented judgment)
Listen for: steps skipped, systems not in the SOP, decisions made differently by different people

**Minutes 45–55 — Delegation signals**
Goal: assess which parts of the work could be handled by an agent.
Questions: (4–5 questions on codifiability, exception rate, risk, reversibility, and latency)
Listen for: whether decision rules can be articulated, how often exceptions occur, what happens when something goes wrong

**Minutes 55–60 — Close**
Goal: confirm key facts and flag anything that needs written follow-up.
Questions: (2 questions to validate what you heard and identify gaps)

---

**SECTION 3 — ATX dimension probes**

For each ATX dimension below, produce 2 targeted questions specific to this scenario. These are backup questions — use them if the natural conversation does not surface the information.

**Volume and time** — how much work, how often, how long per case
**Cognitive nature** — where judgment lives, what takes training, what follows a pattern
**Data and systems** — where data comes from, how many systems, what is manual vs automated
**Risk and compliance** — consequences of error, regulatory requirements, audit needs
**Organisational** — handoffs between teams, approval gates, stakeholder dependencies

---

**SECTION 4 — Evasion and contradiction watch**

Based on this scenario, list 3 specific things the stakeholder might say that sound like an answer but are not. For each, provide a follow-up question to push past it.

| What they might say | Why it is not an answer | Follow-up question |

---

**SECTION 5 — Post-call synthesis prompts**

After the call, paste your notes and run each of the following:

1. "Summarise these discovery notes, identifying: (a) the core processes discussed, (b) all pause points and judgment calls, (c) exception rate signals, (d) risk and latency constraints, (e) any contradictions or evasion detected."

2. "Based on these notes, draft an ATX delegation suitability score for each process discussed. Score each on: input structure, decision determinism, tool coverage, context complexity, exception rate, latency constraint, and risk. Assign a delegation archetype."

3. "What did I not ask that I should have? What gaps remain in my understanding of this scenario?"
```

---

**Expected output:** 5 sections as above. Questions must be specific to the scenario — not generic. Section 2 should be usable as a printed call guide.
